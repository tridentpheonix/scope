PROJECT: Hometown Hardware Website Refresh
SOURCE: Discovery Call Notes - April 10

The client wants to "modernize" their current site. They mentioned it feels "clunky" and "old."

Key notes:
- They have about 20 pages right now but might want to add more, like individual location pages for their 3 stores. 
- They aren't sure who is going to write the new copy yet. Maybe their niece who does social media? Or we can just "tweak" what they already have.
- They have some photos but said some are "just okay" and they might need to take new ones later.
- Mentioned they might want a "form or something" so people can request quotes for lawn mowers.
- They hope to have it done in about six weeks before their big summer referral campaign starts.
- "We are easy to work with, just give us some quick feedback and we can iterate fast. We don't need a formal review process, just send us some edits."

RISKS TO FLAG:
- Vague page count (location pages expansion)
- Unclear copy ownership (niece vs tweaking)
- Asset readiness (mixed quality photos)
- Hidden integration (quote request form)
- Timeline pressure (six weeks/campaign deadline)
- Loose revision boundaries (quick feedback/no formal review)
